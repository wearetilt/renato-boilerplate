Allyship is a personal
responsibility and
life-long journey.

It is the practice of empathizing with the experiences of systemically disadvantaged groups, looking out for biases and actively using a voice and power to advnace equity.

Allyship is not a destination or a self-assessed title; others must see an individual as an ally.

Discover what it means to practice allyship, and consider how comfortable you are with these allyship behaviours.

Disrupt the
status quo

Aware

Observes and understands the pervasiveness of oppression, can identify it, and seeks out information to proavtively fill gaps in knowledge.

Accountable

Holds peers, self, and organization accountable by practicing integrity and actively seeking to challenge and eliminate inequities.

Bold

Respectfully intervenes in non-equitable and non-inclusive situations. Challenges orthodoxies and behaviours that maintain the status quo.
